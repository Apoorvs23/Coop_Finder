      Dreaming big is in our DNA. It’s who we are as a company. It’s our culture. It’s our heritage. And more than ever, it’s our future. A future where we’re always looking forward. Always serving up new ways to meet life’s moments. A future where we keep dreaming bigger. We look for people with passion, talent, and curiosity, and provide them with the teammates, resources and opportunities to unleash their full potential. The power we create together – when we combine your strengths with ours – is unstoppable. Are you ready to join a team that dreams as big as you do?

      COMPANY:

      Founded over 175 years ago, Labatt is one of Canada’s founding businesses and its leading brewer. We are proud of our history and our heritage in Canada, and we remain committed to brewing great-tasting, high-quality beers that have satisfied beer drinkers for generations. Labatt has created exciting experiences with consumers through iconic brands like Budweiser, Bud Light, NÜTRL, Michelob Ultra, Corona, Stella Artois and many more. Through our broad portfolio, we are truly a national brewer, with over 3,600 employees, a portfolio of more than 60 quality beers and beyond, and 10 breweries from coast-to-coast, we are proud to serve Canada and the communities we call home.

      Brewery Internship details:

      The Brewery Trainee Internship is an intensive 4 month program that attracts the brightest, most driven individuals who can take on challenging projects. A Brewery Trainee Intern will work directly with our Supply group. Our Supply group is responsible for brewing, packaging and shipping the famous Budweiser beer. Paramount to everything they do, commitment is to safety and social responsibility. 99.8% of everything that enters our breweries if either completely used in the process or recycled. We also dedicate those big, high-speed lines to help our communities during time of disaster by packaging cans of emergency drinking water. Our customers expect the best and the Supply team provides it.

      We are looking for candidates who are in it for the long-haul. This internship could lead you to an opportunity to join us full-time after you graduate into one of our full-time trainee programs. These include, but are not limited to, our Global Management Trainee Program, Supply Chain Trainee Program, Brewery Trainee Program, and TechnologyTrainee Program.

      • This internship is located in Edmonton, AB ***

      As a Brewery Trainee Intern, you will:

      • Be placed within our Maintenance, Packaging, Brewing, Quality, and Safety departments at our Edmonton Brewery.
      • Own and deliver on a strategic project that is impactful to the business.
      • Develop and demonstrate an understanding of each aspect of the function through multi-functional exposure by liaising with stakeholders in each group.
      • Build and present a final project presentation to Canada's Senior Leadership team.
      • Participate in internship-related events and activities.

      Internship Requirements:

      • You are in your 2nd or 3rd year of a Bachelor’s Degree in Engineering, Business, Science, or a related field, and are continuing your studies in 2024, or are graduating in 2025.
      • You have strengths both working independently as well as part of a team.
      • You consider yourself to be a self-starter with the ability to prioritize.
      • You thrive in a fast-paced environment and you are passionate to deliver results.
      • Analytical thinker and consider yourself a strong problem-solver.
      • Mobility to move across Canada for your potential future role is a key requirement of the position.

      WHY LABATT:

      As one of Canada’s Top 100 Employers, Labatt is a place where our people can bring out the best in themselves through a collective purpose of creating A Future With More Cheers. We are always looking to serve up new ways to meet life’s moments, dream big to move our industry forward, and make a meaningful impact in our communities. We believe in brewing up a future that everyone can celebrate and share.
