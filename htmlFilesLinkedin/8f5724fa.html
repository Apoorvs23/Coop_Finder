      We're looking for a highly motivated co-op student to join our Health Care Programs department.

      Health Care Programs is responsible for the procurement, implementation, and quality management of clinical outcomes and performance measures, as well as monitoring the program costs, of approximately 70 health care contracts. We do this through close collaboration and strong working relationships with both internal stakeholders, such as Claims and Rehabilitation Services, Procurement Services, Analytics and Data Services, Health Care Operations, and Medical Services, as well as with our external provider network and associations.

      This is an (8) eight-month full-time coop term position starting in January 2024.

      H ow you'll make a difference

      You will assist the psychological assessment program team with administrative aspects of referral processing and placement, projects/tasks, and data analysis to support the overarching goal of improved program efficiency. We're in the early stages of planning a Psychology Education Series aimed at providing education to around 90 providers, promising a mix of challenge and reward. Additionally, you'll have the chance to contribute to projects like revamping our provider database and helping develop onboarding resources. If you're looking for a dynamic role with impactful projects, this could be the perfect fit for you!

      Where you'll work

      At WorkSafeBC, we offer a hybrid work model that combines the convenience of working remotely with the dynamism of working in one of our offices, based on the operational needs of the position.

      In these roles, you'll work primarily from your B.C. home and approximately 1 day/month in our Richmond office.

      • Review psychological assessment referrals according to specific parameters, after they have circulated in the portal for a specific timeframe to support timely referral placement.
      • Assist with manual processes for referral placement with specific psychologists.
      • Directing issues to Quality Assurance Supervisor (QAS) to liaise with claims staff when there are booking problems (travel/interpreters/lack of worker contact)
      • Assisting with projects / events on an ad hoc basis (such as assisting with reinstating educational programming and outreach sessions with providers
      • Reviewing data and collating/analyzing and developing monthly reports

      Is this a good fit for you?

      We're Looking For a Person Who Can

      • Demonstrate strong interpersonal skills, with strong written and verbal communication
      • Demonstrates strong critical and analytical thinking skills and a great attention to detail
      • Demonstrate Effective time management skills; ability to prioritize tasks in a fast-paced administrative environment
      • Demonstrate ability to work independently as well as with a virtual team
      • Basic competence and experience with Excel, Word, Power Point etc.
      • Demonstrate ability to engage professionally with internal and external stakeholders
      • Demonstrate strong teamwork

      Your background and experience

      • Enrolled in a Health Care Administration or Psychology program or similarly related program
      • Working Knowledge of Microsoft Office, specifically Word, Excel, PowerPoint.
      • Exceptional attention to detail required.
      • Ability to engage professionally with a wide variety of audiences and stakeholders (internal + external)

      Learn more: Hear from past co-op students on what it's like to work here.

      Important to know

      Before we can finalize any offer of employment, you must:

      • Confirm you're legally entitled to work in Canada
      • Confirm you're a registered student at a post-secondary institution and is participating in a work integrated learning program
      • Consent to a criminal record check and successfully meet the criminal record check clearance

      WorkSafeBC's COVID-19 Employee Mandatory Vaccine Policy (the "Policy") is suspended effective January 9, 2023, however we reserve the right to re-implement it in response to changes in the public health landscape, including public health orders. We are committed to the protection, health, and safety of our employees and our Communicable Disease Prevention Program and related protocols remain in effect.

      Who We Are

      At WorkSafeBC, we promote safe and healthy workplaces across British Columbia. We partner with workers and employers to save lives and prevent injury, disease, and disability. When work-related injuries or diseases occur, we provide compensation and support injured workers in their recovery, rehabilitation, and safe return to work. We're honoured to serve the 2.49 million workers and 263,000 registered employers in our province.

      What's it like to work at WorkSafeBC?

      It's challenging, stimulating, and rewarding. Our positions offer diversity and opportunities for professional growth. Every day, the work we do impacts people and changes lives. What we do is important, and so are the people we do it for.

      Our ability to make a difference relies on building a team with a rich variety of skills, knowledge, backgrounds, abilities, and experiences that reflects the diversity of the people we serve. We are committed to fostering a welcoming, inclusive, and supportive work culture where everyone can contribute as their best, authentic self.

      Our Benefits

      As a member of our team, you'll have access to services and benefits that help you get the most out of work - and life. Along with a competitive salary, your total compensation package includes:

      • 4% in lieu of benefits
      • 4% in lieu of vacation

      Learn more: Discover who we are .

      Salary: $22.38/hourly
